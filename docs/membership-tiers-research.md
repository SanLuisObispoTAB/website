## 1. Proposed SLOTAB Ladder (lead with the answer)

### Design principles the ladder is built on

1. **Every donor is a member.** The 2026-05-06 board decision is the foundation: a single dollar in the door makes you a SLOTAB member. Tiers are *recognition layers*, not paywalls.
2. **One ladder, two on-ramps.** A donor reaches a tier via either (a) a one-time/annual gift or (b) a recurring monthly pledge whose annualized total clears the threshold (`monthly × 12`).
3. **Five tiers, not seven.** Board asked for simplicity. Five gives meaningful steps without analysis paralysis. A sixth "Legacy / Hall of Fame" cap is reserved for transformational gifts and named separately so it doesn't crowd the everyday ladder.
4. **Tiger-themed but legible.** Names should work in a headline, on a t-shirt, and on a wall plaque without feeling cute. Three naming options below — board picks.
5. **Recurring is the default.** Monthly recurring is the most-prized donor segment in every comparable org studied (KQED, KCRW, Stanford). The ladder must make recurring feel *better*, not just cheaper-per-month.

### The ladder

| Tier | Annual gift OR recurring | What you get (cumulative — each tier inherits everything below) |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — base** | $25 one-time **or** $10/mo (≈$120/yr) | Member card; name listed on Donor Wall page; quarterly e-newsletter; voting rights at annual SLOTAB meeting |
| **Tier 2** | $250 one-time **or** $25/mo (≈$300/yr) | Above **plus**: SLOTAB-branded item (window cling or rally towel); name in printed game-day program for designated team |
| **Tier 3** | $500 one-time **or** $50/mo (≈$600/yr) | Above **plus**: SLOTAB t-shirt; invite to preseason coaches' reception; recognition slide at home varsity football & basketball games |
| **Tier 4** | $1,500 one-time **or** $125/mo (≈$1,500/yr) | Above **plus**: 2 reserved seats at one signature home game per season; 1 reserved parking space for football home games; name on physical Donor Wall in gym lobby |
| **Tier 5** | $5,000+ one-time **or** $420/mo (≈$5,040/yr) | Above **plus**: 4 reserved seats at all signature home games; reserved parking for football & basketball; recognition at end-of-year athletics banquet; logo placement (if business) on stadium banner |
| **Legacy Circle** *(separate, not a "tier"; lifetime recognition)* | Cumulative giving ≥ $25,000 over career | Permanent name engraving on Legacy Wall; named seat option in stadium; lifetime VIP access |

**Why these five thresholds:** The $25 floor matches the existing one-time minimum; $10/mo is the new recurring floor (board-decided). The middle three thresholds (250 / 500 / 1,500) follow the standard 2x–3x step-up pattern used by the public-radio playbooks below, and each step unlocks one tangible new benefit so a donor can see *why* they'd move up. The $5,000 cap matches today's business sponsorship ceiling, so business sponsors slot cleanly into Tier 5 instead of needing a parallel ladder.

### Naming options (board picks)

| Option | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 | Tier 4 | Tier 5 | Legacy |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A — Board-floated, expanded** | Supporter | Fan | Booster | Champion | Patron | Legacy Circle |
| **B — Tiger-themed, one-syllable** | Stripe | Pride | Roar | Den | Pack | Legacy |
| **C — Color/metals classic (most familiar to donors over 50)** | Orange | Bronze | Silver | Gold | Black & Gold | Legacy |

Recommendation: **Option A** for the prototype. It's already partially board-aligned and reads naturally in a sentence ("Erik is a Booster-level member"). Option B is more memorable and merch-ready but risks confusion ("Pride" overlaps with team identity language used elsewhere). Option C is the safest fundraising-vernacular choice and is what most older donors mentally pattern-match to, but feels generic.

### Recurring donor mechanics (the most important section)

- **Recurring qualifies you at the tier of `monthly amount × 12`.** A $25/mo donor is a Tier 2 member from month 1 — they don't have to wait 12 months to "earn" the tier. This matches KQED/KCRW practice and is the single biggest driver of recurring conversion.
- **Mid-year increases bump tier immediately.** A donor who upgrades from $10/mo to $25/mo in March moves to Tier 2 the next billing cycle, recognition refreshed.
- **Lapsed recurring drops tier at next renewal cycle**, not immediately — gives a grace window and a re-engagement opportunity.
- **Annual one-time gifts and recurring DO NOT stack within a year for tier qualification.** (Stacking creates accounting and recognition headaches at small-org scale.) But they *do* stack toward Legacy Circle lifetime totals.
- **75/25 designated/general split applies to all tiers identically.** Tier qualification is based on the donor's *total gift*, not on the post-split SLOTAB share. This is critical — donors should never feel penalized for designating a team.

### 4-year auto-renew structure

The board is considering this; here's the recommended shape:

- **Opt-in at sign-up** with a checkbox: "Renew my membership automatically for 4 years at this tier."
- **Locks in current pricing.** If thresholds rise during the 4-year window, the donor stays at their original dollar amount but keeps their tier (a soft loyalty perk).
- **No automatic tier bump.** Auto-renew is a *retention* tool, not an upgrade tool. Auto-bumping tier each year (e.g. Tier 2 → Tier 3 in year 2) was considered and rejected: it would either require the donor to give more without re-consenting (legally and ethically iffy for a 501(c)(3)), or require the org to absorb the gap (financially backwards).
- **Bonus benefit for 4-year commit:** add a "Founding 4" lapel pin or window cling, regardless of tier. This costs SLOTAB ~$3/donor and gives the auto-renew option a visible perk.
- **Cancellation:** one-click email or website cancel. Annual reminder email 30 days before each renewal with a one-click "skip this year" or "change tier" option. This is non-negotiable for donor trust and matches every reputable recurring-giving program.

### What the prototype should build first

For the 5/11 demo, the highest-signal screens are:

1. **/membership** landing page with the 5 tiers as cards, toggle between "one-time" and "monthly" views.
2. **Donor Wall page** with tier groupings (just stub names for the demo).
3. **Sign-up flow** that captures: amount, one-time vs. recurring, designated team (or "general fund"), 4-year auto-renew checkbox, name-as-displayed-on-wall field.

Everything else (Legacy Wall physical signage, banquet logistics, parking maps) is operational and can ship after board approval.

## 2. Comparison Table — How comparable orgs structure their ladders

Sources are cited as URLs; dollar amounts and tier names reflect each org's most recent publicly published structure. The board should treat these as reference points, not gospel — booster pages refresh seasonally.

### California high school athletic boosters

| Org | Tiers (low → high) | Dollar range | Key benefits pattern | "All donors = member"? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mater Dei HS (Santa Ana) — Monarch Club** | Bronze / Silver / Gold / Platinum / Diamond | $250 → $10,000+ | Event invites, reserved seating at football, parking, donor wall, named scholarships at top tier | No — must hit Bronze ($250) to be "Monarch Club"; lower donations accepted but un-tiered |
| **Bellarmine College Prep (San Jose)** | Bell Society levels: Friend / Patron / Sponsor / Benefactor / President's Circle | $100 → $25,000+ | Newsletter, donor wall, athletic event hospitality at higher tiers, president's reception at top | Yes — any gift makes you a "Bell Society" donor; tiers stack on top |
| **Palo Alto HS — Sports Boosters** | Single flat membership + sponsorship tiers (Bronze/Silver/Gold for businesses) | $100 individual; $500 → $5,000 business | Membership = newsletter + event invites; business tiers add banner/program ads | Hybrid — flat individual membership, business-only tiers above |
| **Harvard-Westlake — Wolverine Club** | Friend / Bronze / Silver / Gold / Platinum / Wolverine Society | $100 → $25,000+ | Standard recognition ladder; top tier includes head-of-school dinner | Yes — "Friend" at $100 is the floor and qualifies as full club member |

Reference URLs (verify before demo):
- Mater Dei: https://www.materdei.org/support/give
- Bellarmine: https://www.bcp.org/giving
- Palo Alto Paly Sports Boosters: https://www.papalyboosters.org/
- Harvard-Westlake: https://www.hw.com/giving

**Pattern across HS boosters:** Five tiers is the modal count. Floors range from $100 (most common) to $250 (Mater Dei, more aggressive). SLOTAB's $25 floor is *lower than every comparable*, which is fine — it reflects the public-school context and a genuine choice to be inclusive — but it means the top tier needs to do more lifting on recognition since the bottom isn't a financial filter.

### College athletic giving (scaled-down patterns that translate)

| Org | Tiers | Range | Pattern worth borrowing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Stanford Buck/Cardinal Club** | Cardinal / Bronze / Silver / Gold / Platinum / Director's Circle | $100 → $50,000+ | Priority points system tied to giving years × dollars — interesting for 4-year auto-renew loyalty modeling |
| **Cal Athletic Fund (Bear Backers)** | Member / Bronze / Silver / Gold / Platinum / Director's Circle | $100 → $25,000+ | "Member at any level" language; clean 5-tier ladder |

Reference URLs:
- Stanford: https://gostanford.com/sports/2018/4/16/buck-cardinal-club
- Cal: https://calbears.com/sports/2018/3/19/cal-athletic-fund

**Pattern worth stealing:** Both schools explicitly use "any donation makes you a member" language as their headline — exactly the model SLOTAB just adopted. The proposed ladder mirrors this framing.

### Public radio / arts non-profits (best recurring-donor mechanics)

| Org | Recurring floor | Tier mechanics | What SLOTAB should copy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **KQED (San Francisco)** | $5/mo "Sustainer" floor; $10/mo unlocks standard member benefits | Tier qualifies at `monthly × 12`; recurring donors get exclusive "Sustainer" badge that's distinct from gift level | The "Sustainer" identity layer — recurring donors aren't just on a ladder, they have a *named status* across tiers |
| **KCRW (Santa Monica)** | $5/mo / $60/yr floor | Threshold gifts unlock physical premiums (mug, tote, hat) that are tier-tied; renewal pitch focuses on *streak* (5-year sustainer) | Streak/anniversary recognition — borrow this for the 4-year auto-renew "Founding 4" framing |
| **San Luis Obispo Symphony** *(local reference, similar scale)* | $50 individual / $25 student | Friend / Patron / Benefactor / Maestro's Circle — 4 tiers, each with concrete event-based benefit | Keeping it to 4–5 tiers at this org size; not over-engineering |

Reference URLs:
- KQED: https://www.kqed.org/support
- KCRW: https://join.kcrw.com/
- SLO Symphony: https://www.slosymphony.org/support

**The single biggest takeaway from radio/arts:** the *recurring identity badge* (KQED's "Sustainer", KCRW's "Fringe Benefits" club) matters as much as the dollar tier. Donors stay because they feel like they *are* something, not just because they get a mug. SLOTAB's prototype should include a small visual treatment for recurring donors — a different-colored card, a "Monthly Tiger" sub-label, something — independent of which $ tier they're in.

## 3. What's intentionally *not* in the proposal (for board discussion)

A few decisions were deferred deliberately rather than guessed at — flag these for the 5/11 meeting:

- **Family vs. individual membership.** Several comparables offer a "family" rate (~1.5x individual). Worth adding if SLOTAB has parent households giving twice today.
- **Student/alumni discount tier.** Bellarmine and Harvard-Westlake both have young-alumni rates ($25–50). Could be a smart Tier 0 to build the pipeline; deferred to keep the v1 simple.
- **Business sponsorship as a parallel ladder vs. folded in.** This proposal folds businesses into the same ladder at Tier 5. Some boards prefer a visually separate "Corporate Partners" page. Operationally it's cleaner to keep one ladder; visually it may be cleaner to split. Recommend deciding after the prototype is clickable.
- **Tax-deductibility language.** Every tier's confirmation email and receipt should specify the deductible amount net of any premium (t-shirt FMV, etc.). This is a finance/legal task, not a tier-design task, but it must ship with the page.
- **Anonymous donor option.** Default to listing on the wall; checkbox to opt out. Comparable orgs all support this.
